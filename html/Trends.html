Trends

There are four major trends in hacking.


Nation-State Based Hacking
This form is conducted by governments and is generally kept a secret. Governments use hacking against other governments or to manage their own populations. Governments hire highly qualified coders to do their dirty work. When governments hack each other, it is called cybersabotage or cyberespionage.

Cybersabotage can be used to take control of another governmet's cyber network. One of the most famous examples is the Stuxnet worm, created by the United States, that infilitrated Iranian state computer networks. This worm allowed the United States to control the centrifuges that enriched uranium and caused them to spin at irregular speeds.

Cyberespionage is used to spy on other governments and steal state/industry secrets.

When governments aren't hacking other governments, they are managing their own populations. They create programs that collect invasive amounts of personal information, then use profiling techniques to identify individuals. It has been revealed that China, United Kingdom, and the United States all use similar programs on their citizens. In the west, these techniques are typically used for the "War on Terror."
