Hackathons

So what exactly is a Hackathon?
It is a combination of the words "hacking" and "marathon," which implies an intense, uninterrupted period of programming. Specifically, it is an event where groups of people come together to create software prototype in an alloted time period.


Tell me more!
The purpose of a hackathon can range, but most follow the same structure and characteristics. They are typically centered around a certain technology or theme, such as finance or iOS development. The winning team of the hackathon receives prizes or the opportunity to network.


A hackathon can be internal or external.
For example, Facebook has internal hacking events to motivate employees.
Microsoft holds the International Women's HAckathon, which is an external event to motivate women to get involved with software engineering.


Before a hackathon begins, the team meets to collect ideas.
Once it begins, the team's main focus is to create a presentable version of their ideas.
Affter the alloted time period is over, the team presents their ideas to a panel of judges.
Most of these presentations will be prototypes and not fully functional, because the main object is to see the demo's concept and value. The best presentations are the ones that are easy to understand, and demonstrate the true potential of the idea.

A hackathon enables bottom up and external colloborative ideation since they are allowing anyone to come up with creative ideas and prototype. These ideas can then be used by a company or external third parties. Many ideas from a hackaton are ideas not yet heard of or thought possible, so a hackathon allows these ideas to emerge. Hackathons are also verye ducationl. Even if an idea is unused, a participant gets an educational and social experience. They learn about software engineering, while meeting new people within the team, or within the company or third party who is hosting the event.
